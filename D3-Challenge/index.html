Age, Poverty and Income vs Healthcare, Smoking and Obesitas by State


Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income


In the interactive chart above, the results are shown from a 2014 survey by the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System.

A clear correlation is found between heath risks like obesitas and smoking versus median income and poverty rate.

Almost 25% of Texans lack heathcare, though their poverty rate is about average compared to other states. A clear relation between median income and smoking is also visible. On average, the higher the median income, the less people smoke. This trend is less obvious when comparing median income to obesity, yet it is still significant.
